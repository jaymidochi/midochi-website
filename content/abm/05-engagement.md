---

### Stage 1. Alignment (free)

Before anything else, we need to know if there's a real fit, for both sides. This is a working conversation, not a pitch.

What happens: we look at your go-to-market setup, your revenue data, and your team structure. We work through the questions that determine ABM readiness. Is there real alignment between marketing, sales, and leadership on why you'd do this? Is the time and team you'd need actually available, or just available on paper?

What you get: a clear answer, including "not yet" if the team isn't ready, plus what would need to change first. If there's a fit, you get a pilot proposal: which cluster, what goal, what team, roughly what it takes.

No cost, no obligation. The point is to not waste anyone's time on a program that's set up to fail before it starts.

### Stage 2. Pilot (1-3 months)

This is where the process above gets put into motion, scoped down deliberately so it can move fast.

What happens: a small team, usually 1 marketer, 1 SDR, and 1 subject-matter expert from your side, working closely with us, runs the full process against one market, one cluster, one goal. We call this the 1-1-1-1-1-1-1-1-10 approach: one market, one cluster, one tier, one goal, one source of intent data, one warm-up tactic, around 10 prioritized accounts. The constraint is the point. It forces focus and produces proof without a platform purchase or new hires.

What you get: a validated (or revised) ICP and cluster, playbooks your team has actually run, account-by-account progress on a defined set of accounts, and often, early opportunities from the program itself.

### Stage 3. Recurring partnership (ongoing, reviewed roughly every 6 months)

Once the pilot proves the model on a small scale, the focus shifts from "does this work" to "how does this become how we operate."

What happens: we document the playbooks that worked, help build the internal processes and training so your team can run the motion without us, and apply the same process to new verticals, markets, or tiers. Roughly every 6 months, we review results, reset the goal, and decide what to scale, change, or drop.

What you get: a repeatable, cross-functional ABM motion your team owns, built on a process already tested on your accounts, your data, your buyers.
