title: How we build your program

This is the methodology: the actual sequence of work, whether your team runs it, we run it, or it's a mix during a pilot.

| Step | Description |
| --- | --- |
| Define goals | 1. New pipeline 2. Expansion 3. Acceleration 4. Renewals |
| Deal analysis | Look at your best deals from the last 12-24 months and identify the process to close them |
| Account selection and research | Segment your account list into three groups: Cluster ICP, Future pipeline, Active focus |

| Measure and iterate | Set leading and lagging indicators tied to pipeline and revenue |

### Step 1. Goal

Before any targeting or tactics, we figure out what the program needs to produce. ABM can drive four outcomes: new pipeline, expansion, acceleration, or renewals. The one you pick determines almost everything else.

What we do: break your revenue target down by motion, then check it against your team's actual capacity. How many accounts can you realistically cover with the people and time you have?

What comes out of it: one specific goal. Not "more pipeline." Something like "10 new opportunities from Tier 2 exchanges accounts this quarter."

### Step 2. Deal analysis

Most ICPs are built on guesses. This step replaces guesses with your own revenue data.

What we do: look at your best deals from the last 12-24 months (fastest closes, highest value, best-fit customers) alongside your closed-lost deals. From there, we find the cluster, the group of accounts dealing with the same challenge as your best customers. Then we build qualification criteria (what makes an account a good fit) and disqualification criteria (the patterns that show up in deals that never should've been chased).

What comes out of it: a cluster and an ICP grounded in how your deals have actually closed.

### Step 3. Account selection and research

Now: which specific accounts, and what do we actually know about them?

What we do: segment your account list into three groups.

1. Cluster ICP accounts fit the profile but don't know you yet.
2. Future pipeline accounts show some awareness, but the need isn't confirmed.
3. Active focus accounts are engaged with a validated need. For the highest-priority accounts, we map the buying committee: who's involved, their role, their KPIs, their challenges.

What comes out of it: a prioritized, segmented account list, plus buying committee maps for the accounts that matter first.

### Step 4. Build the touchpoints

This is where the program becomes visible: the content, messaging, and outreach the buying committee actually sees.

What we do: match your value proposition to each buying committee member's goals. Build playbooks aligned to where each account sits in the buyer journey: cluster nurture for awareness-stage accounts, 1:1 development for validated-need accounts, opportunity development for accounts that have declared intent. We go beyond LinkedIn, email, and ads: content hubs, content collaboration, micro-events, and direct mail all have a role depending on the account and stage.

What comes out of it: a documented playbook per segment, with clear ownership split between marketing and sales.

### Step 5. Measure and iterate

A program is only as good as its feedback loop.

What we do: set leading indicators tied to pipeline and revenue (account engagement, account-to-pipeline ratio, pipeline velocity), and put a weekly review in place to look at progress account by account.

What comes out of it: a reporting rhythm that shows what's working before the quarter ends, not after.
